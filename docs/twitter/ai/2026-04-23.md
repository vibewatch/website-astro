### 1.2 ICLR 2026: Agent Evaluation and Human-AI Collaboration Research (new)

A cluster of research from ICLR 2026 in Brazil addressed core challenges in AI agent evaluation, safety, and collaboration. [@michaelryan207](https://x.com/michaelryan207) [presented AutoMetrics](https://x.com/michaelryan207/status/2047295691702083876) (30 likes, 24 bookmarks, 2,223 views): with fewer than 100 human feedback points, it generates automatic evaluation metrics that beat hand-crafted LLM-as-a-Judge rubrics by up to +33.4% in correlation to human judgments.

![AutoMetrics ICLR 2026 paper: 4-step process generating interpretable automatic metrics from human feedback, with top auto-generated criteria including Prohibited Content Avoidance 26.3% and Prompt Optimized LLM Judge 23.6%](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HGlzBvFWwAAr2mB.jpg)

[@Diyi_Yang](https://x.com/Diyi_Yang) [shared the Stanford lab's full ICLR lineup](https://x.com/Diyi_Yang/status/2047298359442948534) (61 likes, 3,204 views): seven papers spanning "The Ideation-Execution Gap" (LLM-generated vs human research ideas), "Searching for Privacy Risks in LLM Agents via Simulation," "How Dark Patterns Manipulate Web Agents," and "Collaborative Gym" for human-agent collaboration evaluation.

![ICLR 2026 poster schedule: 7 papers from Stanford on human-AI collaboration, agent evaluation, privacy risks, and dark patterns](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HGl2iQ9WoAADS8T.jpg)

[@_akhaliq](https://x.com/_akhaliq) [highlighted LLaDA2.0-Uni](https://x.com/_akhaliq/status/2047297426680123630) (26 likes, 13 bookmarks, 4,209 views), the #1 Paper of the Day on HuggingFace: a unified discrete diffusion language model that combines multimodal understanding and generation through a semantic tokenizer, MoE backbone, and diffusion decoder.

![LLaDA2.0-Uni: #1 Paper of the Day on HuggingFace, unifying multimodal understanding and generation with diffusion LLM](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HGl2Hl-WAAApnDP.png)

**Discussion insight:** The AutoMetrics four-step process (generate candidate criteria, retrieve from MetricBank, fit regression, emit importable metric) addresses a structural gap: human feedback is expensive, but LLM-as-Judge rubrics correlate poorly with actual human preferences. The research output from this ICLR suggests the field is shifting from model capability to evaluation infrastructure.

**Comparison to prior day:** April 22 covered SWE-CI and Opik for coding agent evaluation. Today's ICLR research broadens the evaluation focus beyond coding to include content quality, agent privacy, and human-AI collaboration.

## 6. New and Notable

### ARTEMIS: AI Pen-Testing Agent Outperforms 9 of 10 Human Security Professionals

A Stanford/CMU/Gray Swan AI paper presents the first comprehensive evaluation of AI agents against human cybersecurity professionals in a live enterprise environment (~8,000 hosts across 12 subnets). ARTEMIS, a multi-agent framework with dynamic prompt generation and automatic vulnerability triaging, placed second overall with an 82% valid submission rate, outperforming 9 of 10 human participants. Cost: $18/hour for ARTEMIS variants vs $60/hour for professional penetration testers. Key limitations: higher false-positive rates and struggles with GUI-based tasks. [@p_misirov](https://x.com/p_misirov) [noted](https://x.com/p_misirov/status/2047307251778752809) (6 likes, 249 views): "A custom harness architected with proprietary know-how will beat any frontier model on results."

### EvoSkill: Agents That Evolve Other Agents

[@bafspot](https://x.com/bafspot) [described EvoSkill v1](https://x.com/bafspot/status/2047340966982394022) (8 likes, 82 views) from Sentient: a closed-loop system where a coding agent runs benchmarks, studies failure traces, and an evolution engine proposes and tests new skills and prompts. No human prompt engineering required. Results: +7.5pt on OfficeQA, +12.1pt on SealQA, +5.3pt on BrowseComp. The architecture represents a shift from human-designed agent improvement to automated meta-optimization.
